TREKKING TO SOURCE OF GANGA: GAUMUKH/GANGOTRI

FOR CLASS VIII ONWARDS

Duration: 5  Days Max Altitude: 4912 Mts. Best Time: April to November Grade: Moderate Trek

Celebrated in Hindu mythology and religion, the Garhwal Himalayas are said to be the "Abode of the Gods". The river Ganges takes its source in this remote massif, at the sacred shrine of Gangotri and rushes down to the plains in a torrential fury. A trekkers delight, this itinerary leads to one of the most important spots of Hindu spirituality, where you will experience a strange atmosphere of religious fervour. Thousands of Pilgrims arrive here to wash away their sins in the holy Ganga, it is said that Lord Shiva sat at the base of this fall to take  the Ganga into his locks, for otherwise her descent from the heavens would have split the earth. 

From Gangotri, the path proceeds at first along the sacred river, then through dense forests and grassy areas, close to the Source of the Ganges, you find the strange view of half-naked saddhus in the lotus position, meditating on the bank of the bubbling stream pouring out the glacier. Further up, Tapovan is a grassy area with numerous brooks, surrounded by snowy peaks.  

SKETCH ITINERARY

DAY 1: Arrival Haridwar

Arrival at Haridwar Rail/Bus Station. Be greeted by tour guide who shall accompany you throughout this tour. Coach transfer to hotel/camp. Rest of the day free to enjoy the vicinity and sightseeing. Recharge your batteries by making a grand camp fire and cultural show in evening. Overnight at hotel/camp.

 

DAY 2: Haridwar – Uttarkashi 1150 m (195 km)

08.00 am       : Checkout post early breakfast and Drive to Uttarkashi. The drive along the ridge line to Chamba, affords panoramic views of the high mountain peaks of Garhwal. Then down to Tehri to join the Bhagirathi river valley, which we follow up to Gangotri. Overnight in Camp/hotel in Uttarkashi.

 

DAY 3: Uttarkashi–Gangotri (3048 m)–Bhojbasa (3350 m). Drive 97 km/Trek 14 km

Leave hotel after early breakfast and drive 97 km/3 hours. Following the torrential river up stream, the road winds up the valley past the hot sulphur springs at Gangnani, to climb steeply up a series of hairpin bends to Gangotri.

At Gangotri clean your body and mind alike with a holy dip in the sacred river - The Ganges, also called Bhagirathi at its origin. Perform Pooja before lunch.

Post lunch undertake a 14 km soft trek to Bhojbasa. The wide trail initially passes through dense deodar forests before emerging into the wide unforested valley. It then carries on to a small isolated forest of pine at Bhojbasa, literally the abode of pines.

Camp on a grassy meadow besides the Bhagirathi.                                Overnight in camp.
 

GANGOTRI:  This sacred shrine, situated at an altitude of 3,200 Mt is the source of the river Bhagirathi.  The mountain peaks of shivling, satopanth and Bhagirathi sisters surround it.  Accessible by a motor able road, it is one of the most pious Hindu pilgrimages.

THE ETERNAL GIFT OF BHAGIRATH"S PENANCE

{a} TEMPLE OF GANGOTRI: As the legend goes, Raja Bhagirath used to worship Lord Shiva at the sacred stone near which the temple is located.  It is believed that Goddess Ganga first descended to earth from heaven, at this stone.

Amar Singh Thapa, the Gorkha Commander of Garhwal, in the 18th century AD constructed this temple.  It is also believed that the Pandavas came here to perform the great " Dev Yagna" to atone the death of their kinsmen in the battle of Mahabharat.

 

{b} SUBMERGED SHIVLING: Submerged in the river, this natural rock shivling is the place where, according to mythology, Lord Shiva sat when he received the Ganga in his matted locks.  It is visible in winter months when water level decreases.

  

DAY 4: Bhojbasa - Gaumukh (3850 m) - Bhojbasa. Trek 4 km one way

Continue along the pilgrim grassy hillsides. Trail then curves, to unfold a breathtaking view of wild flowers studded meadows crowned by the Bhagirathi group of peaks Camp on a wide sandy beach at Gaumukh, where the holy Ganga gushes out of the snout of the Gangotri glacier, is set in a wide moraine-stream valley Shivling 6540 m, sits looking down like an old learned man, while the Bhagirathi I, II and III tower above. Trace your footsteps back to Bhojbasa camp.                                      Overnight in camp.
 

DAY 5: Bhojbasa - Gangotri - Uttarkashi. Trek 14 km/Drive 97 km one way

Trace back your footsteps and experience the mesmerizing Himalayas once again. Drive back to Uttarkashi. Stop enroute to have glimpse of the winter abode of Gangotri Shrine- The Mukba Temple then at the heaven on earth- Harshil. Stop again to enjoy a most refreshing hot bath in holi Rishikund (Gangani). Overnight at Utarkashi.

 

DAY 6: Uttarkashi–Haridwar

08.00 am  : Pack your bags and drive back to Haridwar post breakfast. Lunch enroute.

04.00 pm  : Arrival Haridwar.       

Tour ends upon reaching at Haridwar.
